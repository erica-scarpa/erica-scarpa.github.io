Two reference collections automatically generated using Zotero and <span class="latex">L<sup>a</sup>T<sub>e</sub>X</span>, unsorted or ordered by author-date. These PDFs are meant for those who prefer a printed version of the additions to on-line bibliography on the *Ebla Digital Archives Project*. The online searchable edition is available on the *EbDA* website, and regularly updated (visit [ebda.cnr.it](http://ebda.cnr.it/biblio)).

For a list of the latest addition please see this page. For mistakes, errors, or omissions, please e-mail me at [erica.scarpa@gmail.com](mailto:erica.scarpa@gmail.com).

## EDIT #1 (2020-02-29)

**ResearchGate feature**: I uploaded the two documents also on [ResearchGate](https://www.researchgate.net/profile/Erica_Scarpa3). Note that ResearchGate has a feature that shows all references included in a document on the "References" panel in the document view. If the document has been uploaded to ResearchGate by the author(s), this panel provides direct access to it.
![](/assets/images/research-gate.png)

<br>
A similar option exists on academia.edu, but I have been increasingly annoyed by its click-baiting-like e-mails, and I am not going to update the page any longer, for several reasons (see for example [[1]](https://www.forbes.com/sites/drsarahbond/2017/01/23/dear-scholars-delete-your-account-at-academia-edu/#5ae200c02d62), [[2]](https://kfitz.info/academia-not-edu/), [[3]](https://www.wired.it/internet/web/2018/02/28/spam-mail-academia/?refresh_ce=), and [[4]](https://btfp.sp.unipi.it/it/2016/01/researchgate-e-academia-edu-non-sono-archivi-ad-accesso-aperto/)). Most of these issues affect ReasearcGate as well, but at least the latter is not spamming his users with tons of e-mails promoting paid contents subscriptions.
