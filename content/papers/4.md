##### Abstract

The Great Archive L.2769 contains a significant number of documents that make repeated mentions of the sons of the Eblaite kings, referred to as "dumu-nita en." These references often group them together and highlight their roles as recipients of various goods during specific events, such as the wedding between Tabūr-damu and Yiṯġar-damu. Previous studies by Alfonso Archi, Maria Giovanna Biga, and Lucio Milano (1988) have identified and compiled several lists of these royal sons, providing valuable insights into the familial structure of the kings and viziers at Ebla. However, recent publications have brought forth previously unknown data, expanding our understanding of kin relationships within the court of Palace G. This note aims to review and update the information presented in Archi, Biga, and Milano's work (1988, 222-232) pertaining to the dumu-nita en.
