##### Abstract

The cuneiform texts found within the Royal Archives of Ebla in Syria, dating back to the 24th century BC, provide a remarkable glimpse into the city's economic, social, and political life. Predominantly, the archives house documents of an economic, administrative, and diplomatic nature, which offer invaluable insights for reconstructing the city's political and economic history. Among these records, there are also a smaller number of literary texts such as spells and hymns, as well as lexical texts encompassing monolingual and bilingual lexical material. The objective of this research is to propose a model of Eblaite society by utilizing the data present in the Royal Archives' documentation. To achieve this, the study employs keywords derived from the Ebla texts that designate various professional categories. Through the application of a sociological and socio-historical methodology, a model is developed that enables an understanding of the levels of interaction and hierarchical structure characterizing different elements within Eblaite society. Each key term undergoes a comprehensive analysis from lexical, administrative, and historical perspectives, contributing to the delineation of the primary components comprising the social fabric. By synthesizing the data collected through this analysis, the research aims to present a comprehensive overview of Ebla's social organization during the middle of the 3rd millennium BC.
